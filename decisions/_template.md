## Context

What is the situation that requires a decision? What forces are at play?
State the problem clearly enough that a reader encountering this ADR cold
understands why a decision was needed.

---

## Decision

What is being decided. State it declaratively.

---

## Options Considered

### Option A: [Name]

Description. Why it was or was not chosen.

### Option B: [Name]

Description. Why it was or was not chosen.

**For retroactive ADRs:** If this decision was made before governance existed,
the options may not have been formally evaluated. Be honest about that.
An "Adoption Context" section may be more appropriate than fabricated
alternatives.

---

## Consequences

What changes as a result of this decision? Include:

- What becomes possible that wasn't before
- What constraints are introduced
- What other artefacts are affected (principles, glossary, registry, specs)
- What governance debt is created or resolved

---

## Failure Criteria

Under what observable conditions would this decision be considered wrong?
Name specific, testable signals — not vague dissatisfaction. These are the
triggers for reconsideration and potential supersession.

mechanically verifiable — a reader should be able to confirm completion
without subjective judgment.

- [ ] Gardener commit to the governance repository
